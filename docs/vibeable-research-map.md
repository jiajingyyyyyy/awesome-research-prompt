# Vibeable Research Map

This document defines which parts of research are good candidates for prompt-vibing.

## Principle

A research part is "vibeable" when quality improves by adaptive framing rather than fixed wording.

## Research-Scope Placeholders

- `{{research_stage}}`
- `{{artifact_type}}`
- `{{decision_risk}}`
- `{{required_rigor}}`
- `{{time_budget}}`

## Stage Map

## 1) Problem Framing
- Vibeable: yes
- Why: framing quality depends on context and constraints

## 2) Paper Reading and Relevance Filtering
- Vibeable: yes
- Why: relevance depends on your project claim and baselines

## 3) Hypothesis and Experiment Planning
- Vibeable: yes
- Why: trade-offs are context-dependent

## 4) Section Drafting
- Vibeable: yes
- Why: structure and tone need adaptive control

## 5) Reviewer Simulation and Rebuttal Strategy
- Vibeable: yes
- Why: attack surface depends on claim-risk profile

## 6) Numeric Result Reporting
- Vibeable: partially
- Why: language can be vibed, but numbers and facts are hard constraints

## 7) Final Submission Compliance
- Vibeable: partially
- Why: checklist logic is fixed, explanation style can vary

## Non-Negotiable Rule

Vibing can adapt wording and workflow, but it cannot alter factual evidence.
All claims must remain evidence-aligned.
